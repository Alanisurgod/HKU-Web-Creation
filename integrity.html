Academic integrity: follow your course, then the University

Understandable navigation means we do not paraphrase disciplinary rules here. Use the official links for exact wording, then apply your course’s AI and collaboration instructions on top.

Priority order (simple and robust)

  1. Course syllabus and Moodle announcements for what you may submit and how to cite help.
  2. University-wide policies for the formal definitions that apply across HKU—linked below.
  3. Ask early if a practice feels grey. A two-line email to teaching staff beats a panel later.

Authoritative HKU resources

  • Teaching & Learning — policies and regulations (plagiarism hub)
  • HKU Undergraduate Handbook (official handbook hub)
  • HKU Teaching & Learning homepage

If a link changes, start from hku.hk and search the current handbook or teaching pages.

Plain-language habits (not policy text)

  • Keep drafts, notes, and source PDFs so you can show how work evolved.
  • Label quotations and ideas from others immediately—retroactive citation is painful.
  • When using any generative tool, default to transparency if your course expects it.

Ethics: we do not reproduce HKU policy text. Official pages remain the source of truth.
