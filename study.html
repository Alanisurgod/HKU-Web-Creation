Study with less friction

Satisfaction grows when your week is predictable. These habits emphasise efficiency: fewer re-reads, clearer notes, and honest time estimates.

Habits that scale

Two weekly anchors

  • Weekly review (30–45 minutes): scan each course’s upcoming deadlines and readings.
  • “Next action” notes: end each study session with one sentence: the next concrete step.

Focused blocks

Try 25–50 minute blocks with a visible timer, phone away, and a single task. Between blocks, stand, water, quick walk—avoid “pseudo breaks” on the same screen.

Active recall beats highlighting

Close the book and write questions from headings. Retrieval is the practice that exams reward—not the comfort of neon yellow streaks.

Moodle is where coursework usually lives

HKU Moodle is the University’s Moodle-based learning site (you may see “hkumoodle” in the title). Each course typically has its own space for materials, deadlines, assignment or report submission, online quizzes, and sometimes discussions or group tasks. Check each course’s Moodle page at least as often as you check email during busy weeks.
