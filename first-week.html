Your first week: a calm checklist

Effectiveness here means fewer missed steps. Work top-to-bottom once, then keep the short “common mistakes” list visible for two weeks.

Checklist (do in rough order)

  1. Reach official information first. Open the HKU homepage and bookmark your faculty site and course Moodle pages.
  2. Email, Portal, and Moodle. Make sure university email forwards or inbox rules match how you actually read mail. Sign in to the HKU Portal for personal and study records, then open HKU Moodle (often branded “hkumoodle”) for day-to-day coursework: announcements, readings, assignment uploads, quizzes, and sometimes forum or group work.
  3. Campus access basics. Student card pickup, door access, and Wi‑Fi: follow the official instructions you receive—details change by year.
  4. Timetable reality check. Add rooms, commute buffers, and meal breaks. If something clashes, note the official change process for your programme.
  5. One library goal. Borrow nothing yet—just find one quiet zone and one group zone for future you.
  6. Support map. Save CEDARS contacts for non-academic support and your department’s undergraduate office route for academic questions.

Official shortcuts

  • The University of Hong Kong

  • HKU Libraries

This companion site cannot list year-specific dates. Use faculty emails and Portal announcements as the source of truth.

Common mistakes (error prevention)

Assuming silence means “no work due.” Check Moodle announcements on a fixed schedule.

  • Ignoring spam filters that catch university mail.
  • Buying every textbook before the first lecture—wait for confirmed reading lists.
  • Running on caffeine alone for the first month—schedule sleep like a serious commitment.
